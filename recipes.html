Creamy Mushroom Pasta

20Min / (X Kr.≈X€)

Ingredients

  • 100g of your favorite Pasta
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 onion
  • 150ml heavy cream
  • 200g mushrooms

Steps

  1. Cook your pasta according to the the instructions
  2. Peel and mince your onion and your garlic
  3. Slice your mushrooms
  4. Heat a big skillet on medium heat
  5. Sauté the onion. After about a minute you can add your garlic. This will prevent it from burning
  6. When the onion becomes translucent and the garlic fragrant, add your mushrooms
  7. Sauté them all together and make sure you do'nt overcrowd the skillet. (overcrowding=soggy mushrooms)
  8. When your mushrooms turn golden add your cream
  9. Turn down the heat to low and let the sauce simmer until thickened
  10. Seasons your sauce sauce with salt and pepper
  11. Toss your pasta into the sauce
  12. Garnish with freshly grated pepper (or.. if you are felling extra fancy like me- with parsley

Veganize me: Use plant based soya cuisine instead of cream
Tip: To save even more money you can pick the mushrooms yourself! Mine are from the Skanderborg forest(~20km from Aarhus)

Seitan Wrap

20Min / (X Kr.≈X€)

Ingredients

  • 1 tortilla wrap
  • 50g pipfri (seiten bites)
  • salad mix
  • 1 tomato
  • 2 tbsp Thousand Island Dressing

Steps

  1. Fry your seitan bites in 2tbsp of oil
  2. Spread the dressing on your tortilla wrap
  3. Top generously with your salad mix, your tomato and your seitan bites
  4. Roll up and enjoy!

Bulgur Bowl

20Min / (X Kr.≈X€)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of bulgur
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot/li>
  • 2 spicy green peppers
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 6 mushrooms
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • salt,pepper, paprika, (optional:dried mint, chili)

Steps

  1. Wash your bulgur
  2. Drizzle 1tbsp oil into your heated pot
  3. Sauté the onion, the spicy peppers and your carrot until they're golden
  4. Add the tomato paste
  5. Peel your tomatoes and cut them into cubes
  6. Add them to your veggie mix and let it simmer until the tomatoes are soft
  7. Add the bulgur and top it with 2 cups of water
  8. Season generously with salt, pepper, paprika (and if you are extra fancy with dried mint)
  9. Put a lid on your pot and let it simmer on low heat for 15 minutes

Tip: This recipe is perfect to substitute the veggies I used with whatever you have left in your fridge.

Veggie Dhal With Jasmine Rice

20Min / (X Kr.≈X€)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of jasmine rice
  • 1 cup of red lentils
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 big potato
  • 1/2 broccoli
  • 1/2cup of frozen peas
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 200ml coconut milk
  • salt, pepper, 1tsp turmeric

Steps

  1. Cook the rice according to the instructions
  2. Wash and chop your vegetables in the same size so they're done at the same time
  3. Sauté the onion, the carrot and potato until they're golden
  4. Add the tomato paste
  5. Wash the lentils and add them to your vegetables
  6. Deglaze with 150ml of water
  7. Bring it to a boil and reduce the heat to low
  8. After 15 min check your vegetables and lentils. You can add the coconut milk if they are soften.
  9. Season generouslywith salt, pepper, turmeric (and chili)

Tip: This is enough for two meals. If you don't think you can finish the Dhal you can easily freeze it.

Lentil Soup

20Min / (X Kr.≈X€)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of red lentils
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 big potato
  • 1tbsp tomato paste
  • 1tbsp butter
  • 1/2 lemon

Steps

  1. Chop your vegetables in quarters
  2. Sauté them for 3-5 minutes on high heat
  3. wash your lentils and add them to the vegetables
  4. add 1,3L of water and reduce the heat to low
  5. after 15 minutes you can check if your lentils and vegetables became soft
  6. give it a quick blitz and season it
  7. melt the butter in a separate pan
  8. add the tomatopaste
  9. pour the mixture to the soup and season it with mint
  10. garnish it with mint and serve it with freshly squeezed lemon juice

Tip: Lemon juice in soup sounds weird, I get it. But trust me, it's e s s e n t i a l.
